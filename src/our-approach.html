Partnerships

How do we join forces to create a beneficial ecosystem for all stakeholders ?

- We joined the forces of two industry research and design labs (from EDF and frog) who had respectively worked on physical and digital solutions to have a positive impact and could continue to learn together.

- We called on multiple internal expertise and user tests to progress.

- We are inspired by an ever-growing literature on Regeneration (Kate Raworth, Laura Storm & Gile Hutchison, Julia Watson, Christian Wahl, Carol Sanford, Ellen MacArthur Foundation, Raz Godelnik and many others).

What goals do we strive for ?

- We used a mindfulness approach that starts with reconnecting with oneself and goes step by step toward the ambitious goal of having a positive impact on all forms of life.

- We aim to share this goal and process to create a collective awareness and movement toward Regeneration.

Time

How do I make sure benefits continue to hold and grow through time ?

- This website has a limited lifetime.
It is intended to live a few months, not more. This is enough to create awareness and collect data.
Our result will then be exported as a pdf and stored elsewhere. The server will have space to welcome another website.

- Individual first step, collective awareness.
The individual sentence is ephemeral, yet can be collected and shared by users, as a reminder or step in their journey.
The collective map and the whole process will be studied and published as an academic paper to contribute to build our collective knowledge.

Environment

How do we cancel our negative impacts and create a symbiotic relationship with environmental ecosystems through time ?

- Energy-saving mindful web design
Dark mode design: less energy is used by the pixels in a LED screen.
Open source fonts with a constraint of one typeface in two weight variations for faster load time and space optimisation.
Graphical assets exported as svgs, optimized format for quality and space optimisation.

- Upcycled, solar hosting, http://www.updata.solar/:
Server powered by solar energy, and website is down when battery runs out.
Solar panel and battery powering the server are materials that reached end of commercial life at EDF and are upcycled.

- A domain name with positive social and environmental impact
We chose the .fr domain name because we found that its manager, afnic (https://www.afnic.fr/en/) was transparent about use of funds, pledging 90% of their benefits to local social actions, being active about data privacy and with actions to reduce their carbon impact.

Our approach

Climate emergency requires a change of paradigm.

Regeneration is creating the conditions for life to endlessly renew itself, transform into new forms, and thrive amid ever-changing conditions. (Hutchins & Storm, 2019). It is a North Star that pushes us to not only reduce our negative impact but strive to multiply the benefits of our actions on social and environmental ecosystems. It encourages us to expand our view in time and scope, but also act in mindfulness, step by step but consistently, and avoid giving out to fear and inaction.

Is being at a loss for words preventing us from acting ?

When we started working on how to have a positive social, environmental and climate impact through our design projects, we realized each of us constantly faced difficulties picking the right word, which in turn prevented us from acting. Words take on meaning as they become used, shared, and linked to products, services, activities and actors. Sense making is a preliminary step for individual and collective action. Through this experience, we wish to unveil what the words of the sustainability lexicon means to people beyond their theoretical definition. We hope the process and result helps individuals and communities think and act for regeneration.
