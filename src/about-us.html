FrogLab

frogLab is a provocative agent to challenge frog practices and engage our clients to explore new fields. It was started in 2018 to create visionary projects and iconic demos to show our clients they could be bolder.

We are an interdisciplinary team of PhDs and designers exploring the future, delivering high value assets, iconic prototypes and new ways of working for designers. We work with industry experts, academic research centers and clients in partnership mode, combining the rigor of academic research with the human-centered approach of design.

frogLab is composed of 4 Research Programs and more than 20 projects that help frog stay one step ahead of the design practice. We explore the impact of AI on design, digital humanities, reinventing organizations through design, and regenerative design practices.

edf pulse design

Our mission is to use design to explore the future and emerging uses of carbon neutral energy. We strive to make promising solutions widely accessible while preserving the fragile equilibriums that pre-existed us.

Innovate
In touch with users and context sensitive, we design tangible solutions accessible to the largest number of people. The energy industry is at the center of multiple critical issues, making the practice of design urgent, singular and multifaceted. We work on a daily basis with the group’s various divisions to provide pragmatic, user-centered answers to their challenges.

Explore
By closely listening to emerging trends, we imagine future uses and trigger their development. We look beyond the current market to start original projects based on emerging technologies, practices and phenomena. We take these reflections as far as possible and we embody them in bold and convincing proofs of concept.

Inspire
Detecting weak signals help us sketch out bold and radical new positions, and clear out new paths for the future. We envision ourselves into the future with a lucid and critical look, particularly with respect to our current standing. This invites us to summon the strongest disruptions and spring up the most audacious opportunities. Through this practice, we provoke and borrow from the future its best trajectories.
